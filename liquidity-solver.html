Two-sided inventory desk · Base ⇄ KalqiX

Desk inventory simulation

Replaying real swap flow as if every trade settled directly from on-chain inventory on Base with a mirrored trade on KalqiX.

Rebalance events

Each event moves inventory between the two venues and resets both to the target split — ETH / cbBTC / USDC in 1 : 1 : 2 on each side.

Model assumptions

  • Both venues hold the same book: ETH, cbBTC, and USDC in a 1 : 1 : 2 ratio, half the capital on each side.
  • A buy is paid straight out of Base inventory and the user's USDC joins it; the mirrored KalqiX trade runs the other way, so the combined position across both venues stays constant.
  • The mirrored trade executes at the same price as the Base settlement (no slippage or delay).
  • The quote fee is charged on top of trade size and collected as USDC on Base.
  • A trade goes unserved when the paying venue lacks the outgoing asset — Base for the user's side, KalqiX for the mirror; an unserved trade immediately forces a rebalance.
  • Idle inventory earns nothing; there is no interest income or expense in this model.
  • Every served trade pays a fixed settlement cost — a plain ERC20 transfer on Base, no lending-market call — deducted from Base USDC. Editable in the panel.
  • Rebalance cost = bps on the notional moved between venues (0 by default), plus a fixed cost per event.
  • Net P&L = fees − settlement gas − rebalance costs. Inventory mark P&L (price moves on the structural ETH/cbBTC holding) is reported separately.

Capital

Capital is split evenly between the two venues; each side holds ETH, cbBTC, USDC in 1 : 1 : 2.

Settlement

Each settlement is a plain ERC20 transfer on Base — no lending-market call, so it's cheaper than a solver-style trade. Edit to match measured gas.

Rebalancing

Drift = largest deviation of any leg from its target share, checked on both venues. Trades push the two sides in opposite directions, so drift is what fires.
